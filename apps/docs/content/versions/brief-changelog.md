## v0.3.0 — 2026-03-01
**Capacitor — Launchpad-first positioning** — [Read archived version](/versions/brief/v0-3-0)

Strategic reframe: lead with the shippable product (launchpad + emissions), not the full four-system stack. Capacitor branding covers everything — the governance layer reveals itself over time.

**New positioning:** "The people who build a project should own more of it over time."

**What's new:**
- Launchpad as the primary product pitch (not governance)
- Head-to-head comparison table: Capacitor vs Clanker vs pump.fun
- Three tiers of Work (Default, Custom, Qualitative) with clear progression
- Decay curve explanation with formula
- Qualitative Work teased as "where Capacitor is going next" — governance hinted, not pitched
- The Opportunity section grounded in real market data (Clanker volume, pump.fun volume)

**What's gone from v0.2.0:**
- Full Capacitor Stack framing (Emitter/Capacitor/Facilitator/Executor)
- Governance economics (Capacitor layer details)
- Facilitator / Organization OS
- Executor
- "The Loop" (all four systems composing)
- "A computer that runs as a company" positioning

**Strategic shift:** The adversarial analysis revealed the launchpad is the only part of the stack that ships standalone, has a proven market, and generates revenue on day one. Governance, agent orchestration, and execution are research — not product. Lead with what ships.

## v0.2.0 — 2026-03-01
**Capacitor Stack — Full system brief** — [Read archived version](/versions/brief/v0-2-0)

Complete rewrite covering the full Capacitor Stack: four systems composing into a self-sustaining organization.

**New positioning:** "A computer that runs as a company."

**What's new:**
- Full stack framing: Emitter → Capacitor → Facilitator → Executor
- Emitter layer: token launches, emissions, decay curves, fee splitting, auto-compounding LP
- Facilitator layer: Organization OS, agent registry, context targeting, solicitation
- Executor layer: decision execution (placeholder)
- The Loop: how all four systems feed each other
- Naming normalization: Capacitor (not Capacitr), Emitter (not EmMittr)

**What's gone from v0.1.0:**
- Capacitr Ventures section (will return in a future version)
- Competitive comparisons (vs Snapshot, vs Polymarket)
- Detailed charging/settlement mechanics (moved to Stack docs)
- "How Agents Find Opportunities" section

## v0.1.0 — 2026-02-27
**Capacitr Pivot — Governance-first positioning** — [Read archived version](/versions/brief/v0-1-0)

Complete rewrite of the brief for the Capacitr governance-first protocol.

**New positioning:** "The first protocol where investing in a project means governing it."

**What's new:**
- Governance as the core product (not token launch)
- Capacitor economics: speaking and voting cost tokens, being right earns them back
- The Reflexive Signal: governance quality creates a reflexive market loop
- Bonding curve entry as cold-start solution
- Flat-rate discharge: nobody buys a token they can't get out of
- Capacitr Ventures: fund that invests by governing
- Competitive set: vs Snapshot/Tally/Aragon (governance infrastructure)

**What's gone from EmMittr brief:**
- Emmissions, decay curves, three Work classes
- Three-tier integration story (default/custom/autonomous)
- "Growth engine for token economies" positioning
- Clanker/Doppler/Virtuals/Bittensor competitive comparisons
